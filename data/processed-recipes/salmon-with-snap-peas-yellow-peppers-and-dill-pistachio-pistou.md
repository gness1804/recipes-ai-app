# Salmon with Snap Peas, Yellow Peppers and Dill Pistachio Pistou

Rating: [MISSING]

## Ingredients

### For the Dill-Pistachio Pistou
- 0.33 cup fresh dill, chopped
- 0.33 cup green onions (about 2), finely chopped
- 0.5 cup natural pistachios or almonds, shelled, toasted, finely chopped
- 0.25 cup pistachio oil or extra-virgin olive oil

### For the Vegetables
- 2 yellow bell peppers or orange bell peppers, cut into 0.5-inch strips
- 1 lb. sugar snap peas, trimmed, strings removed
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 0.25 cup water
- 0.5 tablespoon pistachio oil or extra-virgin olive oil

### For the Salmon
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz. each)
- 1 tablespoon pistachio oil or extra-virgin olive oil

## Instructions

### For the Dill-Pistachio Pistou
1. Mix dill, green onions, pistachios, and 0.25 cup oil in a medium bowl.
2. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
3. Pistou can be made 2 hours ahead. Let stand at room temperature.

### For the Vegetables
1. Heat 0.5 tablespoon oil in a heavy large skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Add peppers and sauté until beginning to soften, about 2 minutes.
3. Add snap peas, garlic, and 0.25 cup water; sprinkle with salt.
4. Sauté until vegetables are just tender and water evaporates, 2 to 3 minutes.
5. Stir in 1 rounded tablespoon pistou.
6. Transfer vegetables to a platter; tent with foil to keep warm. Reserve skillet (do not clean).

### For the Salmon
1. Heat remaining 1 tablespoon oil in reserved skillet over medium-high heat.
2. Sprinkle salmon with salt and pepper.
3. Place salmon, skin side down, in skillet; cook until skin is crisp, about 3 minutes.
4. Turn salmon and cook until almost opaque in center, about 3 minutes longer.
5. Arrange salmon fillets over vegetables on platter.
6. Spoon some pistou down the center of each fillet and serve, passing remaining pistou alongside.
