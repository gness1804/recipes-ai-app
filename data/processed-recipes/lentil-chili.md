# Lentil Chili

Rating: 4.5/10

## Ingredients

- 12 oz. to 1 lb. (about 2 to 2.33 cups) brown lentils, dry
- 28 oz. vegetable broth
- 12 oz. water
- 1 can (14.5 oz.) reduced-sodium diced tomatoes
- 1 cup onion, chopped
- 2 cups green bell pepper, chopped
- 4 teaspoons bottled minced garlic
- 1 can (6 oz.) no-salt-added tomato paste
- 2 tablespoons ketchup
- 1 tablespoon chili powder
- 1.5 teaspoons ground cumin
- 0.5 teaspoon dried thyme
- 0.25 teaspoon salt, or to taste
- 0.5 teaspoon ground black pepper, or to taste
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 0.5 teaspoon balsamic vinegar
- Hot pepper sauce, to taste (optional)
- Shredded Cheddar cheese, for serving (optional)
- Sour cream, for serving (optional)

## Instructions

1. Bring broth and water to a boil in a 4.5-quart Dutch oven or soup pot.
2. Meanwhile, pick through lentils checking for stones or debris. Rinse lentils and drain.
3. When broth boils, add lentils, and bring broth mixture back to a boil.
4. Reduce heat to low, and cook at a very slow boil, covered (crack lid to vent if pot threatens to boil over), for 20 minutes.
5. Add tomatoes with juice to pot.
6. Peel and coarsely chop onion, and add to pot.
7. Seed and coarsely chop green peppers, and add to pot.
8. Add garlic, tomato paste, ketchup, chili powder, cumin, thyme, salt, and black pepper. Stir to mix well.
9. Simmer for 15 minutes, covered, stirring frequently. Add a little more water or vegetable stock if all moisture evaporates.
10. Remove pan from heat. Stir in olive oil and vinegar.
11. Add hot pepper sauce to taste, if desired.
12. Serve at once, topped with cheese and sour cream, if desired.
