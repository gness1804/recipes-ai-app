# **Coconut-Ginger Salmon with Green Beans (over Jasmine Rice)**

* **Time:** \~10 min prep, \~20–22 min cook

* **Serves:** 4

* **Why it fits:** Fish-forward, no dairy/eggs, mild heat, not citrus-heavy; simple pantry-led sauce.

**Ingredients (10 or fewer)**

1. Salmon fillets, about 1¼ lb (4 portions)

2. Light coconut milk, 1×13.5-oz can

3. Fresh ginger, 2–3 inches (minced)

4. Garlic, 2 cloves, *optional/reduce if sensitive*

5. Green beans, 12 oz (trimmed)

6. Scallions, 1 bunch (sliced, whites/greens separated)

7. Jasmine rice, 1½ cups (uncooked)

8. Low-sodium soy sauce or tamari, 2–3 Tbsp

9. Ground turmeric, ½ tsp

10. Lime wedges, *optional, serve on the side* (kept light for GERD)

**Steps**

1. Start rice: rinse 1½ cups rice; cook per package.

2. Sauce base: warm 1 Tbsp neutral oil; sauté scallion whites \+ ginger (and garlic if using) 1–2 min. Stir in turmeric, then coconut milk \+ soy. Simmer 3–4 min.

3. Add salmon (skin side down if skin-on) and nestle in green beans. Cover and simmer gently 8–10 min until salmon flakes and beans are crisp-tender.

4. Taste and adjust soy. Serve over rice. Top with scallion greens. Lime on the side for anyone who wants it.
